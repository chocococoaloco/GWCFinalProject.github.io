Savanna Biome

The Savanna Biome is expected to have a change in rain patterns, meaning more dry periods and less annual rainfall in the next decades. The Savanna already doesn't recieve much water: it only gets rainfall during the wet season. Because it relies so much on water resources, the Savanna will be vulnerable when it starts experiencing dry spells. The spells will also make the savanna susceptible to rapid fires that will harm the plants and trees.

Black Rhino


The black rhino is an endangered animal in the savanna because of constant poaching, the loss of their habitat, disease, and the lack of genetic diversity. You can help the black rhinos by contacting government officials in Africa and Asia and talk to them about poaching and stronger. You can also donate to rhino conservation organizations, volunteer for charities, educate others.

African Wild Dog

African wild dogs are a critically endangered species in the Savanna because of a continuous habitat fragmentation, conflict with human activity, and infectious diseases. You can help grow the population of African Wild Dogs by advocating for them and engaging local communities to protect their wildlife; also you can donate to the World Wild Life Foundation.

Giant Anteater

The giant anteater is another endangered species in the savanna because of habitat descrution, fires, and roadkill in addition to being poached for fur and bushmeat. Ways you can help include volunteering for conservation organizations an donating to zoos to support the anteaters.
