Tropical Rainforest Biome

The Tropical rainforest Biome houses millions of species, is normally located close to the equator, and averages 20 to 25 degreed celsius. Some rare species and isolated populations that live in this biome are threatened by the extreme heat waves caused by climate change. This problem expected to turn 30 to 60 percent of the tropical rainforest into a savanna.


Bengal Tiger


Bengal tigers are hunted illegally because their skin and some body parts are considered useful for traditional Asian medicine. Bengal tigers also face population fragmentation, habitat loss, and a decrease of their prey. Also rising sea levels are pushing these tigers out of their habitat as the water claims more and more of their habitat, and causes their freshwater to become saline. You can help them by only buying products from sustainable companies and never buying real fur.

Pygmy Sloth

The Pygmy Sloth is an endangered species because humans are cutting down the red mangrove trees where sloths live, taking away their habitat. The health of sloth populations is connected to the health of tropical rain forests. But tropical rain forests are at risk of deforestation. Without an abundance of trees, sloths will lose their shelter and food source.
